Word Hurdle Strategy Guide: Master the 6-Letter Grid

To excel at Word Hurdle and other daily games like Wordle that use extended word lengths, you need a different strategy than the standard 5-letter approach. Here are our proven techniques:

  • Start vowel-heavy: Words like "AUDIO", "ADIEU", or "OUNCE" reveal multiple vowels in one guess. Since 6-letter words almost always contain at least 2-3 vowels, this gives you a strong foundation.
  • Watch common prefixes: RE-, UN-, IN-, DIS-, and PRE- appear frequently in 6-letter words. If you see these patterns in your feedback, you've already cracked half the word.
  • Look for suffixes: -ING, -TION, -ED, -ER, -LY are your friends. Many 6-letter words follow predictable suffix patterns that dramatically narrow your options.
  • Use high-value consonants: T, N, S, R, L, C in your first 2-3 guesses eliminate the most possibilities. These letters appear in over 60% of English 6-letter words.
  • Don't repeat gray letters: Every wasted guess costs you in a 6-try game. Track your eliminated letters carefully.
  • Think in word families: If you know "BETTER" doesn't work, try "BETTOR", "BATTER", "BUTTER" — changing one letter at a time is more efficient than jumping between unrelated words.
